Tree Identification Guide

Estimating the total number of tree species worldwide is a challenging task due to the vast diversity of ecosystems and the ongoing discovery of new species. On state in 2023 it was estimated that there are approximately 60,000 to over 73,000 different species of trees on the planet.
This number is a dynamic figure, subject to change as botanists and researchers continue to explore remote areas and utilize advances in genetic analysis to identify and classify new species. Additionally, certain regions of the world, such as tropical rainforests, are particularly rich in biodiversity, and many species in these areas are yet to be documented.
It's important to note that the number of tree species can also be influenced by factors such as deforestation, climate change, and other environmental pressures, which can lead to the loss of species or the emergence of new ones.
In the United Kingdom, the total number of tree species, including native, naturalized, and non-native species, is quite diverse, but the exact numbers can vary based on the criteria used for classification. Here's a general breakdown:
Native Species: The UK has about 50 native tree species. These are species that have naturally colonized the British Isles since the last Ice Age and have not been introduced by humans.
Naturalized Species: Naturalized species are non-native species that have been introduced (intentionally or unintentionally) and have established self-sustaining populations in the wild. The number of naturalized tree species in the UK is more challenging to quantify precisely, as it can change over time with new introductions and species adapting to the environment. There are likely dozens of naturalized tree species.
Non-Native Species: The total number of non-native tree species (including those that are not naturalized) in the UK is quite high, as many species have been introduced for ornamental purposes, forestry, or other uses. This number could be in the hundreds, considering all the species grown in parks, gardens, and arboretums, in addition to those that have naturalized.
Total Tree Species: Combining native, naturalized, and other non-native species, the total number of tree species present in the UK could easily be several hundred. This reflects the UK's long history of plant introduction and cultivation.
It's important to note that these figures can vary depending on the source and the criteria used for counting species. For example, some counts might include only those species that are commonly found, while others might list every species recorded, including those that are very rare or have only been grown in very controlled settings, like botanical gardens.
